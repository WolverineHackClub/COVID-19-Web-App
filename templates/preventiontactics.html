Prevention Tactics

Hygiene

Washing your hands before eating, and after using the bathroom, to remain clean. The COVID-19 virus dissolves with soap, but it takes time and rubbing, so be sure to wash your hands with soap for at least 20 seconds.

Masks/Facial Coverings

Selecting a mask: Choose masks that: completely cover your nose and face, fit snugly against the sides of your face, and have multiple layers of breathable fabric. For more details and an in-depth guide, use this cdc website. Surgical Masks and N95 masks are not recommended, as they are intended for healthcare workers and cannot be put at risk of going out of stock.

Wearing it properly: If you are using a mask or cloth, make sure it covers both your nose and mouth Completely. Wearing it over just the nose or mouth will not help, as the virus can still make it in and out of the air and your respiratory system.

Making your own mask: If you are unable to buy one or simply want to make your own, you can follow the steps to this CDC guide to making one!

OTHER IMPORTANT NOTES: DO NOT TOUCH YOUR FACE. Even with a mask on, touching the mask could transfer the virus directly into your respiratory system, completely eliminating the purpose of your mask. Also, do not touch your face in general, as this is a very common way for any sickness to spread to you (including COVID-19)
